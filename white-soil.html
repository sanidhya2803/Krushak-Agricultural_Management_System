    White Soil

    Information:

    White soil is a type of soil found in parts of the Deccan Plateau in India. It is pale or white in color and has a high calcium content, which makes it alkaline in nature. Due to its alkalinity, white soil is not suitable for growing crops that prefer acidic soil. White soil is also used for building construction and road construction due to its high load-bearing capacity.

    Crops suitable for this Soil:

    White soil is alkaline in nature, which means it has a high pH level and is not suitable for growing crops that prefer acidic soil. However, certain crops can still be grown in white soil with the right techniques and management. Here are some crops that are suitable for white soil: Cotton, Jowar (Sorghum), Maize (Corn), Groundnut (Peanut), Sunflower, Soybean, Castor, Sesame, Bengal gram (Chickpea), Pigeon pea (Red gram), etc.

    Soil Locations:

    White soil is primarily found in the peninsular region of India, particularly in the Deccan Plateau. It is found in parts of Andhra Pradesh, Telangana, Karnataka, Maharashtra, and Tamil Nadu. White soil is commonly found in the Vidarbha region of Maharashtra and the Rayalaseema region of Andhra Pradesh. It is also found in parts of Karnataka, Tamil Nadu, and Telangana.

    Climate Condition:

    White soil regions in India have a semi-arid to arid climate, with hot and dry summers and mild winters. The region receives low to moderate rainfall, ranging from 500 to 800 mm annually. The climate is generally characterized by high temperatures and frequent droughts. Despite the harsh conditions, farmers in these regions have adapted to grow crops that can tolerate alkaline soil and drought.
