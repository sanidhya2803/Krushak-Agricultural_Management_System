    Laterite Soil

    Information:

    Laterite soil is a type of soil that is found in many parts of India. It is a reddish-brown soil that is formed by the weathering of rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. Laterite soil is known for its low fertility and high iron and aluminum content. This makes it difficult to grow crops without proper soil management practices, such as adding fertilizers and organic matter.

    Crops suitable for this Soil:

    Laterite soils are good for tea, coffee and cashew cultivation. Laterite soils lack fertility due to intensive leaching. When manured and irrigated, some laterites are suitable for growing plantation crops like tea, coffee, rubber, cinchona, coconut, areca nut, etc.

    Soil Locations:

    In India, laterite soil is found in the Western Ghats, parts of the Deccan Plateau, and in some parts of the Eastern Ghats. It is also found in the coastal regions of Kerala, Karnataka, Goa, Maharashtra, Odisha, and West Bengal.

    Climate Condition:

    Laterite soil is formed by weathering of rocks in warm and humid tropical and subtropical regions with high rainfall. The process of leaching removes nutrients and minerals from the soil, leaving behind a soil enriched with iron and aluminum oxides that gives it a red color. Laterite soil is generally not very fertile and requires careful management and nutrient supplementation for agricultural productivity.
